Tofu Masala

Description

Packed with flavor and extremely satisfying, tofu masala is another standard in our household. The baked tofu also tastes good on salads, so this is almost like a 2-in-1 recipe! Great for cozy rainy or snowy days, a genuine comfort meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp. garam masala
  • 1 1/2 tsp. turmeric
  • 1 1/2 tsp. curry powder
  • 1 tsp. paprika
  • 1 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1 (14 oz.) package extra firm tofu drained, pressed, and cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup greek yogurt
  • 2 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 1/2 sliced or chopped onion
  • 2 Tbsp. tomato paste
  • 4 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 Tbsp. peeled and grated ginger
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 (8 oz.) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (13.5 oz.) can coconut milk
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Cooked basmati rice for serving
  • Optional: Fresh or store bought naan for serving

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400° and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, combine garam masala, turmeric, curry powder, paprika, and salt; mix to combine. In a seperate larger bowl, combine yogurt and half of spice mixture; mix well. Add tofu and gently toss to coat.
  3. Arrange yogurt-coated tofu pieces on baking sheet in a single layer so that they aren't touching. Bake in the center rack for 30 minutes, turning the tofu after 15 minutes.
  4. While the tofu bakes, heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add onions and cook until soft and translucent. Stir in tomato paste, garlic, ginger, and remaining spice mixture. Cook 2 more minutes, stirring often, until the paste turns brick red and begins to stick to the pan.
  5. Add broth, tomato sauce, and coconut milk. Whisk to combine. Gently simmer masala sauce, uncovered, until is reduces and thickens, about 12 to 15 minutes.
  6. Remove tofu from oven and transfer to masala sauce, stirring to coat. Serve over rice and garnish with fresh cilantro.

*To press tofu without a tofu press, place between paper towels, and then between two baking sheets. Place something heavy on top (a pile of books or a bowl full of water) for 10-20 mintues.
